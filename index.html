About Me
I specialized in developing log file-based dose calculation engines for patient-specific quality assurance for Intensity Modulated Proton Therapy (IMPT). In addition, I have worked with three proton centers (Sumitomo, Mevion, and IBA system) to build their log file-based dose reconstruction. My Ph.D. research focuses on developing a supercomputer accelerated log file-based Monte Carlo simulation for proton therapy.

I received the Outstanding Young Investigator award by the Chinese Society of Medical Physics, Taipei, in 2015 and the Outstanding Research Award at the 2019 GREAT Symposium from the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center. I also have published seven peer-reviewed papers and more than ten conference presentations in the past five years.

Get to know me!

Senior medical physics resident with proven research and clinical experience on track for full ABR certification by 2024. I am passionate about workflow optimization as well as safety and quality improvement in radiotherapy. My personal goals are advocating the role of medical physicists and contributing our professional knowledge to public education.
